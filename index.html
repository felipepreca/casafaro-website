— Origen

Every coast has its keepers.
Ours keep one lit.

Named for the lighthouses of México's coast. A single light, kept through the night — for a stranger, for a sailor, for the one still at sea. The oldest hospitality we know.

The bottle takes their shape. The mark — an arched door — is the way in. The light, still on.

— The Vessel

Hand-thrown.
Fired twice. Unlabeled.

Stoneware — the clay of México's coast. Sealed by hand. The mark pressed into the body before firing.

No paper. No glass. Poured slowly. Kept after.
